This research project is motivated by a regulatory tension in AI governance concerning minors. In the EU and UK, regulations such as the Digital Services Act, Age Appropriate Design Code, and EU AI Act require AI companies to proactively engineer safety measures for minor users while simultaneously restricting them from profiling or identifying minors using behavioral data. This creates a bind: simple age verification methods (like checkboxes) are easily circumvented, yet linguistic or behavioral profiling to infer age raises significant privacy concerns. Meanwhile, in the US, companies including Google, Meta, and OpenAI reportedly predict users' ages based on various signals, though these practices remain largely opaque. This project will test whether models like ChatGPT can accurately classify users' ages based solely on input samples (ideally text, but as multimodal communication becomes more prominent, speech as well). Such a capability may already be influencing how AI systems interact with users, potentially shaping safety interventions without transparency or oversight. By surfacing and evaluating this capability, we hope to contribute to informed policy discussions about the trade-offs between child safety and privacy.
